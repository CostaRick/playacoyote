How did I get here?

Where on my way did I lose my energy, courage and joy?

Expectations 

None of us is exhausted from the beginning. We start with hope, energy, desires and enthusiasm. The requirements are growing fast and we learn to handle them. Every hurdle we take makes us tackle new challenges.
But there comes a point where we get tired and despite all efforts, the power is no longer increasing. Tasks that we have done before are troublesome and suddenly become insoluble.
Any athlete would now be at the point where a workout break would be the only solution. But we do not treat ourselves to this break. Others expect performance from us and we expect to fulfill the expectation.

A matter of time 

In the beginning of our career, we usually had a manageable and predictable daily routine. We had a lot of time for our hobbies, family and friends.
And we also had more time for peace and thought. We could start thinking and come to a conclusion. Even insomnia and overworking have their beginning in the flood of expectations ... usually again the own expectation of oneself.

Always available

We are never alone today. But our social contacts are being replaced by social media. We are always connected to hundreds of people who could potentially contact anyone at any time.
At the same time, a mutual pressure is built up to answer private and professional inquiries without delay. If the answer to a message is only delayed by minutes, it is already pressed for an answer. Emails are already terminated by default with a request for quick answers.
With this tug in the networks and tearing of the thoughts, as well as the incessant flood of information and demand for information, our psyche can not keep up for long.

The rise before the fall

We fall behind and can no longer live up to our requirements. We also like to punish ourselves because we feel that people around us are better able to cope with the demands. We run the risk of losing ourselves to the competition. In doing so, we increasingly consume ourselves until the psyche, which has its limits with each of us, no longer participates.

Try to keep for a long time a heavy item with an outstretched arm in front of you. Eventually the muscle gives way to not getting damaged. Why do we accept that with our muscles, but our psyche may not?

The fight against yourself

Our answer is to fight on, to continue to fear, to feel that we have to do justice to others, not to make mistakes, to be always faster and better.
The mistakes accumulate, the pressure increases and the self-doubt, insomnia and depression become stronger. Any message, whether private or business, will add to the burden and fight against your own psyche. We fight against our own limits.

But we can - and will - only win this fight if we accept our own limits!

Find yourself,
the heal burnout strategy

For this we have to get out of the fight and find peace. Quiet without requirement, without time pressure, without pressure to succeed. In a new environment of peace. Here and after recovery time, we will rediscover and define our boundaries. We ground and rediscover ourselves. What are our goals and how we will achieve it lies within us.

We will help you reach out for it.
